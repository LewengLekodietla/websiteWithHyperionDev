What is Business Analysis?

Business analysis is “the practice of enabling change in an enterprise by defining needs and recommending solutions that deliver value to stakeholders. Business analysis enables an enterprise to articulate needs and the rationale for change, and to design and describe solutions that can deliver value” – Business Analysis Body of Knowledge, International Institute of Business Analysis (IIBA), Version 3.0, 2015



A Business Analyst Professional

A business analyst is any individual, in a formal environment, who carries out business analysis tasks. According to the ‘Business Analysis Body of Knowledge business analysts are responsible for:

  • The discovery of information
  • The synthesizing of information
  • The analysis of information

What is the Business Analyst Body of Knowledge?

The BABOK, i.e., Business Analysis Body of Knowledge, is a formal guide that provides comprehensive definition to the profession and duties of the business analysis profession. The guide comprises six knowledge areas that are agreed standards of the business analysis profession:

Knowledge Area A competent Business Analyst rating 1 to 10 (per knowledge area)
Business Analysis Planning and Monitoring 9
Elicitation and Collaborating 9
Requirements Life Cycle Management 9
Strategy Analysis 8
Requirements Analysis and Design Definition 9
Solution Evaluation 9
